A clinical record and dialysis workflow system for a nephrology unit

NCDMS extends the OpenMRS platform with nephrology-specific forms, lab trend tracking, and dialysis unit workflows. It is being built openly for a professorial nephrology unit at a university hospital, and designed so other units facing the same paperwork can adapt it too.

Why this exists

Clinic paperwork doesn't have to live in spreadsheets and Google Forms

A busy nephrology and dialysis unit generates a constant stream of structured data: serum creatinine and eGFR trends, dialysis sessions, vascular access details, transplant work-up status, and referral queues. Much of this currently lives across paper charts and ad-hoc digital forms, which makes it hard to track a patient's trajectory or report on the clinic as a whole.

NCDMS is an attempt to bring that data into a single record system using OpenMRS, an established open-source electronic health record platform used worldwide. Rather than building a new EHR from scratch, we configure and extend OpenMRS with the specific concepts, forms, and dashboards a nephrology unit needs.

What it does

Three things the system is designed around

Clinical record

Structured forms for nephrology visits capture lab trends — serum creatinine, eGFR, haemoglobin — with automatic CKD staging, alongside the standard OpenMRS patient record.

Dialysis workflow

Companion modules support the operational side of a dialysis unit: session scheduling, bed/chair allocation, and a priority queue for patients.

Reporting & registry

A foundation for unit-level reporting and registries — useful both for day-to-day clinical decisions and for research and Ministry of Health reporting.
